03 — The Process
03
Process

How to Introduce
a Client

Every referral follows the same six steps. Getting these right protects your commission, your client, and your standing in the network.

1
Qualify First
Before you mention a deposit, confirm there's genuine interest. Ask if they've been thinking about a tattoo, whether they've struggled to find the right design, and whether bespoke is something they'd consider. Don't pitch to everyone — pitch to the right people.
2
Tease, Don't Teach
Lead with curiosity, not information. The goal is to spark interest, not explain every step. Use the hooks in this pack. Let their questions do the work. When they ask the price, tell them — and mention finance is available.
!
Don't quote specific Affirm rates or terms — direct all finance questions to the No Regrets team.
3
Collect the Deposit
Use the approved payment link or bank details provided by No Regrets Studios only. Do not use your own payment systems.
$
Signature: $500 non-refundable  ·  Premium: $300 non-refundable
4
Pass Client Details
Send the client's full name and contact details to the No Regrets Studios team within 24 hours of collecting the deposit. Your commission is tied to proper client registration — don't delay.
5
Transfer Immediately
Transfer the deposit electronically and immediately to No Regrets Studios' designated bank account. Never hold, retain, or delay any deposit funds for any reason.
!
Retaining client deposits is grounds for immediate termination without notice or further commission.
6
Hand Over & Follow the Journey
No Regrets Studios takes over the client experience. You receive monthly commission statements. Your 5% tattoo commission applies every time that client returns — for life.
04 — Talking Points
04
Hooks

What to Say —
How to Say It

These hooks are designed to feel natural — not like a pitch. Use them on Stories, in captions, or when someone asks directly. Curiosity always outperforms information.

The Fear Angle · For first-timers
"The reason most people don't get a tattoo isn't the pain. It's not knowing what they actually want. This studio fixes that."
Works well for followers who've said they'd "love a tattoo but..." — speaks directly to their hesitation.
The Regret Angle · Plays into the brand name
"I found a studio that shows you your tattoo on your body before any ink touches your skin. No guessing. No regrets."
The Photo Booth is the most shareable part of the experience — this hook leads directly into that story.
The Ownership Angle · The part most people don't know
"You pay for the design, you own it. You can take it to any tattoo artist in the world. They just happen to have incredible artists themselves."
The ownership model is genuinely surprising. Let the reaction be the content.
The Bespoke Angle · For anyone tired of generic
"It's not picking something off a wall. They design something that's actually yours — your idea, your story, your skin."
Resonates with anyone who values personal expression. Strong for lifestyle and fashion audiences.
The Finance Angle · Removes the price barrier
"Didn't know you could finance a tattoo design. Actually makes sense — it's art you'll wear forever."
Use when price comes up in DMs. Reframes the cost without you having to justify it.
What to Avoid
✗  Don't quote the price unprompted in a post
✗  Don't over-explain — curiosity beats information
✗  Don't sound like an ad — personal observations work
✗  Don't promise outcomes, timelines or availability

The Story Sequence

A suggested 3-slide Story flow. Follow this order — hook, reveal, action — and let curiosity do the work.

1
The Hook
Open with a question or a surprising observation. No explanation yet. Make them stop scrolling.
"The reason most people don't get a tattoo isn't pain. It's not knowing what they actually want."
2
The Reveal
Show the Photo Booth moment or the ownership angle. One fact that reframes everything. No pitch.
"You see your tattoo on your body before any ink touches your skin. I didn't know this was a thing."
3
The Invitation
Soft CTA. DM me, link in bio, or a poll. Make it easy to take one step — not a commitment.
"DM me if you want to know more — happy to share details."
